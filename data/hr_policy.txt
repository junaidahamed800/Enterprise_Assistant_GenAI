HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY DOCUMENT
Organization: Apex Solutions Pvt. Ltd.
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Version: 3.2

SECTION 1: EMPLOYMENT AND ONBOARDING

All new employees are required to complete the onboarding process within the first week of joining the organization. This includes submission of identity documents, educational certificates, and signed copies of the offer letter and employment agreement. The HR department is responsible for issuing employee ID cards and system access credentials during this period.

Background verification is mandatory for all full-time, part-time, and contractual employees before the final offer letter is released. The verification process covers previous employment history, criminal records, and educational qualifications. Any discrepancy found during background checks may result in withdrawal of the offer or termination of employment.

Each new hire is assigned a buddy or mentor from their respective team to help them navigate the company culture and processes. The buddy program runs for the first 30 days and includes weekly check-ins. HR conducts a formal onboarding satisfaction survey at the end of the first month to identify and address any gaps.

Probationary periods apply to all new employees for a duration of six months from the date of joining. During this period, performance is monitored through monthly reviews conducted by the direct manager. Employees who do not meet performance expectations during probation may be released from service with two weeks' notice.

Employment contracts clearly define the role, responsibilities, compensation structure, reporting relationships, and terms of engagement. Employees are expected to read and acknowledge all clauses before signing. Amendments to the contract require mutual written consent from both the employee and an authorized HR representative.

SECTION 2: WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE

Standard working hours are from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with a one-hour lunch break. Employees are expected to maintain a minimum of 8 productive working hours each day. Flexible work timings may be granted upon approval from the department head and HR.

Attendance is tracked through a biometric system at the office premises. Remote employees must log in to the official attendance portal at the start and end of each working day. Repeated untracked attendance may trigger a disciplinary review.

Employees are permitted a maximum of three late arrivals per month without a formal warning. A late arrival is defined as reporting to work more than 15 minutes after the scheduled start time. Beyond three instances, a formal written warning will be issued, and continued lateness may affect the employee's performance rating.

Overtime work must be pre-approved by the department manager and recorded in the HR system. Employees in non-exempt categories are entitled to overtime pay at 1.5 times the regular hourly rate. Compensatory time off may be offered instead of overtime pay at the discretion of management.

Unplanned absences must be reported to the immediate manager and HR before 9:30 AM on the day of absence. Employees are required to provide a valid reason and may be asked to submit supporting documentation, such as a medical certificate, upon return. Three or more unplanned absences in a calendar month will be flagged for review.

SECTION 3: LEAVE POLICY

Annual leave entitlement is 18 days per calendar year for permanent employees, accruing at 1.5 days per month. Leave cannot be carried over beyond 9 days into the next calendar year. Unused leave beyond the carry-forward limit will lapse at year end.

Sick leave is granted at 12 days per year and does not carry over to the next year. Employees must submit a medical certificate from a registered practitioner for absences exceeding two consecutive days. Sick leave taken in conjunction with annual leave requires prior approval from HR.

Casual leave is limited to 6 days per year and must be applied for at least 24 hours in advance, except in emergencies. It cannot be clubbed with other types of leave unless approved by the department head. Unused casual leave cannot be encashed or carried forward.

Maternity leave is available to all female employees who have completed at least 80 days of employment in the preceding 12 months. The entitlement is 26 weeks for the first two children and 12 weeks for subsequent pregnancies. Maternity leave pay is provided at full pay for the entire duration as per the Maternity Benefit Act.

Paternity leave is available for 5 working days within 30 days of the birth of a child. It is available for the first two children and must be applied for at least one week in advance. Paternity leave does not carry over if unused.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT

All employees are expected to maintain a professional and respectful demeanor in all workplace interactions. This includes communication with peers, managers, clients, and vendors. Any behavior that creates a hostile or uncomfortable work environment will not be tolerated.

The use of company resources, including computers, internet, and office supplies, must be strictly for business purposes. Personal use of these resources should be minimal and must not interfere with work responsibilities. Unauthorized use of company assets may result in disciplinary action.

Employees must dress in accordance with the company's dress code policy, which requires business casual attire from Monday to Thursday and smart casual on Fridays. Client-facing roles are expected to maintain business formal dress at all times. Exceptions may be granted for cultural or religious observances upon prior approval.

Confidential business information must not be shared with unauthorized parties under any circumstances. This includes financial data, client lists, proprietary processes, and strategic plans. Violation of confidentiality obligations may result in immediate termination and legal action.

Employees are prohibited from engaging in activities that constitute a conflict of interest with the organization. This includes moonlighting for competitors, accepting gifts from vendors above a value of INR 2,000, and entering into personal financial dealings with clients. Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to HR in writing.

SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

The performance review cycle runs twice a year — mid-year in July and annual in December. Employees are assessed against pre-set KPIs, behavioral competencies, and peer feedback scores. Ratings are distributed on a five-point scale ranging from "Below Expectations" to "Outstanding."

Goal setting is done collaboratively between the employee and their manager at the start of each performance cycle. Goals must be SMART — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. The agreed goals are documented in the HR performance management system and serve as the basis for the mid-year and annual reviews.

Managers are trained to conduct performance discussions constructively and objectively. They are expected to provide regular feedback throughout the year, not just during formal review periods. Continuous feedback is encouraged through the use of the internal feedback tool available on the HR portal.

Employees who receive a rating of "Below Expectations" are placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). The PIP outlines specific areas for improvement, measurable milestones, and a 60 to 90-day timeline for demonstrated progress. Failure to meet the PIP goals may result in role change, demotion, or termination.

High performers with ratings of "Exceeds Expectations" or "Outstanding" are considered for fast-track promotions, spot bonuses, and stretch assignments. Recognition programs such as the Employee of the Month award are designed to reinforce high performance. Annual increment percentages are also linked directly to performance ratings.

SECTION 6: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

Salaries are reviewed annually in the month of January, effective from February 1st of each year. Increments are determined based on the annual performance rating, market benchmarking data, and organizational budget. The compensation committee finalizes increment slabs before the appraisal cycle begins.

All employees are entitled to health insurance coverage under the group mediclaim policy. The base coverage is INR 3,00,000 per employee per year, with the option to enhance the sum insured at the employee's cost. Coverage extends to the employee's spouse, dependent children, and parents upon enrollment.

Provident Fund (PF) contributions are made by both the employer and employee at 12% of the basic salary as per the EPF Act. Employees are also covered under the Employee State Insurance (ESI) scheme if their gross salary is below INR 21,000 per month. These contributions are managed and tracked by the payroll department.

The company offers a performance-linked variable pay component for eligible roles. Targets and payout percentages are communicated at the beginning of the financial year. Variable pay is disbursed after the annual performance review, subject to organizational financial performance.

Employees who have completed three or more years of continuous service are eligible for gratuity as per the Payment of Gratuity Act. Gratuity is calculated at 15 days of last drawn salary for each completed year of service. The amount is paid at the time of separation from the company.

SECTION 7: GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL

The organization has a structured grievance redressal mechanism to address employee concerns fairly and confidentially. Employees can raise grievances related to workplace behavior, policy violations, compensation disputes, or managerial conduct. All grievances are treated with seriousness and resolved within 30 working days.

Employees must submit their grievance in writing through the HR portal or via email to the HR Business Partner. Anonymous complaints are also accepted through the ethics hotline. Each complaint receives a unique ticket number for tracking purposes.

The HR team will acknowledge the grievance within 48 hours of receipt. An investigation is initiated within 5 working days and conducted by a neutral HR representative or committee. Both the complainant and the accused party are given an opportunity to present their perspectives during the investigation.

If the grievance is found to be valid, appropriate corrective action is taken, which may include counseling, warnings, suspension, or termination depending on the severity of the violation. The complainant is informed of the outcome in writing. Retaliation against employees who raise genuine grievances is strictly prohibited.

Employees who are dissatisfied with the resolution may escalate the matter to the Chief People Officer or the Ethics Committee. A final decision will be communicated within 15 additional working days. For matters that cannot be resolved internally, employees may seek external remedies through appropriate legal channels.

SECTION 8: LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT

The company is committed to continuous learning and allocates a dedicated training budget for each employee annually. Employees can use this budget for job-relevant courses, certifications, workshops, and conferences. Requests for external training must be submitted to HR at least 30 days in advance for approval.

A mandatory induction training program is conducted for all new joiners within the first two weeks of employment. The program covers company values, compliance requirements, IT systems, and role-specific technical content. Completion of induction training is tracked and forms part of the onboarding checklist.

Functional and leadership development programs are organized quarterly by the L&D team in collaboration with department heads. These programs are aligned with the company's strategic competency framework. Attendance and participation are factored into the year-end performance evaluation.

The company maintains a digital learning platform where employees can access self-paced courses at any time. The platform offers over 500 courses across technical, behavioral, and leadership domains. Completion of courses is tracked and displayed on the employee's learning dashboard.

Employees who wish to pursue long-term educational programs such as MBA or specialized certifications may apply for the education assistance program. The company provides up to INR 50,000 per year as reimbursement for approved programs. Recipients are required to remain with the organization for at least one year after course completion.

SECTION 9: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

Disciplinary action may be initiated for violations of company policies, misconduct, poor performance, or unethical behavior. The process follows a progressive discipline model to ensure fairness and due process. Each step is documented and maintained in the employee's HR file.

The first level of disciplinary action is a verbal warning, issued by the immediate manager in a private setting. The conversation and the warning are documented in writing and shared with HR for record-keeping. A verbal warning remains on record for 6 months.

If the behavior or performance issue persists, a written warning is issued by the HR department in consultation with the manager. The written warning clearly outlines the violation, expected corrective behavior, and the timeline for improvement. A written warning remains on record for 12 months.

In cases of gross misconduct, such as fraud, harassment, or theft, the company reserves the right to proceed directly to suspension or termination without following the progressive steps. An inquiry committee is constituted to investigate such cases within 72 hours. The employee may be placed on suspension with pay during the investigation period.

Termination as a disciplinary measure is a last resort and requires written approval from the HR Director and the Business Unit Head. The employee is provided with a termination letter citing the specific reasons for separation. Exit formalities, including the final settlement and relieving documentation, are completed within 30 days of the termination date.

SECTION 10: EXIT MANAGEMENT

Employees who wish to resign must submit a formal resignation letter to their manager and HR with the required notice period. The notice period is one month for junior and mid-level employees and three months for senior management roles. Serving the full notice period is mandatory unless a buyout is agreed upon in writing.

An exit interview is conducted by HR for all departing employees to understand their reasons for leaving and gather feedback on the work environment. The information collected is treated as confidential and used only for internal improvement purposes. Exit interview data is reviewed quarterly by the HR leadership team.

Employees must complete a full and proper handover of all responsibilities, documentation, and pending tasks before their last working day. The handover must be signed off by the reporting manager and submitted to HR. Failure to complete the handover process may delay the issuance of the relieving letter.

Company property, including laptops, access cards, mobile phones, and any other issued equipment, must be returned by the employee on or before the last working day. All company data must be deleted from personal devices and accounts upon separation. Failure to return company property may result in deduction from the final settlement.

The full and final settlement, including pending salary, leave encashment, and other dues, will be processed within 45 days of the last working day. The relieving letter and experience certificate will be issued after the completion of all exit formalities. References for future employment will be provided in accordance with the company's reference policy.

SECTION 11: ANTI-HARASSMENT AND WORKPLACE SAFETY

The organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward any form of workplace harassment, including sexual harassment, verbal abuse, bullying, and intimidation. All employees have the right to work in an environment that is free from hostility and discrimination. Any act of harassment, whether by a manager, peer, or external party, will be met with swift disciplinary action.

Sexual harassment is defined broadly to include unwelcome physical contact, sexually colored remarks, display of explicit material, and quid pro quo demands. The company's Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) is constituted as per the Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act and is responsible for receiving and adjudicating complaints. The ICC comprises a majority of women members and is chaired by a senior female employee.

Employees who experience or witness harassment are strongly encouraged to report it immediately to the ICC or the HR Business Partner. Reports can be made verbally or in writing and will be treated with complete confidentiality. Retaliation against anyone who files a complaint in good faith is a disciplinable offense.

The company conducts POSH awareness training for all employees at least once a year. Leadership teams receive specialized training on their responsibilities under the POSH Act and relevant state government orders. Training records are maintained by HR and submitted to the District Officer as required by law.

The organization also maintains a safe physical work environment by adhering to all applicable occupational health and safety regulations. Emergency evacuation procedures are prominently displayed throughout the office, and fire drills are conducted twice a year. All incidents involving workplace injury or near misses must be reported to the facilities and HR teams within one hour.

SECTION 12: REMOTE WORK POLICY

Remote work arrangements are available for roles that allow work to be performed effectively outside the office environment. Employees must seek prior written approval from their manager and HR before transitioning to a remote work arrangement. Approvals are granted based on role suitability, individual performance track record, and team operational requirements.

Remote employees are expected to maintain the same productivity standards and availability as their in-office counterparts. Core working hours of 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM must be observed for meetings, collaboration, and responsiveness. Employees must be reachable on all company-approved communication tools during these hours.

Company data accessed during remote work must be handled with the same level of security as on-site work. Employees must connect to the company VPN at all times when accessing internal systems remotely. The use of personal email accounts or unauthorized cloud storage for company data is strictly prohibited.

Remote employees are responsible for maintaining an appropriate and professional work environment at their home or remote location. This includes ensuring adequate internet connectivity, a quiet workspace for calls and meetings, and proper ergonomic setup. The company may provide a one-time work-from-home allowance to eligible employees for setting up their remote workspace.

Performance of remote employees is tracked based on output and deliverables rather than hours logged. Managers are trained to manage distributed teams effectively and to use digital collaboration tools for visibility. Regular one-on-one meetings between remote employees and their managers must occur at least weekly.

SECTION 13: DIVERSITY, EQUITY, AND INCLUSION

The organization is committed to building a diverse and inclusive workplace that values and respects individuals regardless of gender, age, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or socioeconomic background. Diversity is viewed as a strategic strength that drives innovation, creativity, and better decision-making. All employment decisions, including hiring, promotion, and compensation, are made on the basis of merit and qualification.

The company actively promotes gender diversity at all levels of the organization, including at the senior leadership level. Recruitment teams are trained to identify and eliminate unconscious bias in the hiring process. Gender-neutral job descriptions, diverse interview panels, and structured evaluation criteria are used in all hiring rounds.

An annual Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) report is published on the company intranet, sharing progress metrics and areas for improvement. The DEI committee, composed of employee volunteers and HR representatives, meets monthly to review initiatives. Employees are encouraged to participate in DEI programs, employee resource groups, and inclusion workshops.

Accommodations for employees with disabilities are made available to ensure equitable participation in the workplace. This includes accessible office facilities, adaptive technology, flexible work arrangements, and customized onboarding support. Employees requiring accommodations must submit a formal request to HR, which will be handled with complete confidentiality.

SECTION 14: SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

Employees are free to maintain personal social media accounts but must be mindful of content that could impact the company's reputation. Any post, comment, or share that could be perceived as representing the company's views must be avoided unless explicitly authorized by the communications team. Employees must not identify themselves as company representatives in personal social media content without approval.

Confidential company information, client details, financial results, and internal communications must never be shared on social media platforms. Even seemingly harmless operational details could constitute a breach of confidentiality when shared publicly. Employees are reminded that social media posts are permanent and traceable, even after deletion.

The company's official social media accounts are managed exclusively by the marketing and communications team. Employees must not post on behalf of the company or respond to public queries directed at the organization without authorization. Misuse of company branding or impersonation of official accounts will be treated as a serious disciplinary matter.

SECTION 15: EMPLOYEE WELLNESS PROGRAM

The organization offers a comprehensive employee wellness program designed to support physical, mental, and emotional health. Key offerings include an Employee Assistance Program (EAP), which provides free and confidential counseling services to all employees and their immediate family members. Employees can access up to 6 free counseling sessions per year through the EAP provider.

Fitness reimbursements of up to INR 5,000 per year are available for expenses related to gym memberships, sports activities, or fitness equipment. Employees must submit receipts through the HR portal to claim this reimbursement. The program is designed to encourage healthy lifestyle habits and reduce long-term health risks.

Mental health awareness campaigns are conducted quarterly to reduce stigma and promote psychological well-being. The company hosts webinars with mental health professionals on topics such as stress management, work-life balance, and resilience. A dedicated mental health month is observed in October with structured activities and resources for all employees.

SECTION 16: EMPLOYEE REFERRAL PROGRAM

The company encourages employees to refer qualified candidates for open positions through its Employee Referral Program (ERP). Referral bonuses are provided to employees whose referred candidates are selected and complete three months of service in the organization. Bonus amounts vary by role level and are communicated in the ERP guidelines published on the HR portal.

Employees participating in the referral program must not misrepresent the company or the role to potential candidates. Any coercion, pressure, or manipulation of a referral is grounds for disqualification from the program. The hiring decision for referred candidates rests entirely with the recruiting team, independent of the referring employee's influence.

Referral bonuses are paid through the standard payroll cycle and are subject to applicable taxes. Employees who have resigned or been served a termination notice at the time of the referral bonus payment are not eligible to receive it. The HR team maintains an up-to-date referral tracker to ensure transparency in the process.

SECTION 17: TRAVEL POLICY

Business travel must be pre-approved by the department head and submitted through the travel request portal at least 5 working days before the planned travel date. Travel bookings are handled by the company's designated travel management partner to ensure cost compliance and duty-of-care obligations. Personal bookings for business travel must be avoided as they may not be reimbursed.

Domestic travel entitlements are defined by employee grade level, covering economy class flights, AC train travel, and approved hotel categories. International travel is permitted only for senior management and above unless specifically approved for junior staff. All international travelers must carry a valid company travel insurance card issued by HR.

Travel expense claims must be submitted within 7 working days of return from travel with original receipts for all expenditures above INR 500. Cash advances for travel are available upon request and must be reconciled with actual expenses within 10 working days. Unreconciled advances are deducted from the employee's next payroll cycle.

SECTION 18: POLICY COMPLIANCE AND AMENDMENTS

All employees are responsible for reading, understanding, and complying with the HR policies of the organization. Ignorance of the policy is not accepted as a justification for non-compliance. Policy acknowledgment is required annually and forms part of the employee's compliance record.

Policy violations are handled through the disciplinary process described in Section 9. The severity of the action depends on the nature, frequency, and impact of the violation. HR maintains a confidential record of all policy violations and disciplinary actions.

This policy is subject to change at the discretion of the organization, with reasonable prior notice provided to employees. Significant changes will be communicated via official email, team meetings, or HR portal announcements. Employees are encouraged to raise questions or suggest improvements to HR policies through the HR helpdesk or suggestion portal.

All HR Policy sections have been formally reviewed and approved by the HR Director and Legal Counsel.
