LEAVE POLICY DOCUMENT
Organization: Apex Solutions Pvt. Ltd.
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Version: 2.8

SECTION 1: POLICY OVERVIEW AND OBJECTIVES

This leave policy establishes the framework for managing employee time-off entitlements across all categories of leave available within the organization. The policy ensures that employees can maintain a healthy work-life balance while meeting business operational requirements. It applies to all permanent, contractual, and probationary employees unless otherwise specified.

The objective of this policy is to standardize leave practices across all departments and geographies where the organization operates. Uniform application of the policy promotes fairness and transparency in how time off is granted and managed. Any exceptions to the policy must be formally approved by the HR Director.

Leave management is handled through the self-service HR portal, where employees can apply for, track, and cancel leaves in real time. Managers receive automated notifications and must respond to leave requests within 48 hours. The system auto-updates leave balances upon approval or rejection by the reporting manager.

All leave types are subject to adequate advance notice unless the nature of the leave prevents it, such as in the case of a medical emergency. Employees are expected to plan and apply for leaves responsibly to avoid disruption to team deliverables. Leave that causes operational risk may be deferred by management with valid justification.

This policy is reviewed annually by the HR team to ensure compliance with applicable labor laws and alignment with evolving organizational needs. Amendments are communicated to all employees through the official internal communication channels. Employees are responsible for familiarizing themselves with the latest version of the policy.

SECTION 2: ANNUAL LEAVE (PRIVILEGE LEAVE)

Annual leave, also referred to as Privilege Leave (PL), is provided to allow employees to rest, recharge, and attend to personal matters. The annual entitlement is 18 days per calendar year for permanent employees who have completed their probationary period. Probationary employees are not entitled to annual leave until confirmation of employment.

Annual leave accrues at the rate of 1.5 days per completed calendar month. Employees must ensure their leave balance is sufficient before applying for annual leave. Applications must be submitted at least 5 working days in advance to allow for adequate planning within the team.

A minimum of 5 consecutive days of annual leave must be taken at least once per year for wellness purposes. Employees who do not take this minimum annual break may be mandated to take it by the HR department. This is in alignment with the organization's commitment to employee health and mental well-being.

Annual leave can be carried forward to the next calendar year, subject to a maximum carry-forward limit of 9 days. Any balance beyond the carry-forward limit will lapse at the end of the calendar year and cannot be encashed. Employees are encouraged to plan their annual leave usage to avoid unnecessary lapsing of entitlements.

Leave encashment of annual leave is permitted at the time of separation from the organization for employees with a minimum of 1 year of completed service. Encashment is calculated on the basis of the last drawn basic salary. Annual leave encashment is not available during the course of employment unless permitted under special organizational schemes.

SECTION 3: SICK LEAVE

Sick leave is provided to support employees who are unable to attend work due to illness or injury. All permanent employees are entitled to 12 days of sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not accrue and is not carried forward to the next calendar year.

Employees must inform their reporting manager and the HR department of their absence due to illness before 9:30 AM on the first day of absence. Notification can be made via phone call, email, or the HR portal. Failure to notify may result in the absence being marked as unauthorized leave.

For sick leave of more than two consecutive days, employees are required to submit a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner upon return to work. The certificate must indicate the nature of illness, the period of recommended rest, and the fitness to resume work. HR reserves the right to seek a second medical opinion if deemed necessary.

Sick leave taken immediately before or after a public holiday or weekend may require additional documentation to confirm its genuine nature. Management may question the pattern of sick leave usage if recurring on Mondays, Fridays, or around holidays. Misuse of sick leave is treated as a disciplinary matter.

In cases of hospitalization or extended illness, sick leave entitlement may be exhausted, and the employee may apply for leave without pay. HR may also consider advancing sick leave from the next calendar year's entitlement in exceptional circumstances, subject to approval by the HR Director. All extended sick leave cases are handled with sensitivity and confidentiality.

SECTION 4: CASUAL LEAVE

Casual leave is intended for short, unplanned personal requirements that do not fall under sick leave. Employees are entitled to 6 days of casual leave per calendar year, available from the date of joining. Casual leave cannot be accumulated and lapses at the end of the calendar year.

Casual leave must be applied for at least 24 hours in advance in non-emergency situations. In genuine emergencies, the employee must inform their manager as early as possible on the day of absence. Retroactive approval of casual leave without prior intimation may be declined at the manager's discretion.

A maximum of 2 consecutive days of casual leave may be taken at a time. Taking more than 2 consecutive days requires the employee to apply for annual leave instead. Managers are advised not to approve casual leave that is clearly for extended personal vacations.

Casual leave cannot be clubbed with annual leave without explicit approval from the HR Business Partner. Combining casual and annual leave creates a longer absence that may need advance planning and manpower coverage. Managers should assess operational availability before approving such combinations.

Unused casual leave at the end of the calendar year will lapse and is not eligible for carry-forward or encashment. Employees are encouraged to utilize casual leave through the year for genuine personal needs. The HR system will send reminders in November to employees with unused casual leave balances.

SECTION 5: MATERNITY LEAVE

Maternity leave is a statutory entitlement under the Maternity Benefit Act and applies to all female employees who have been in continuous service for at least 80 days in the preceding 12 months. The leave entitlement is 26 weeks for the first two deliveries. For the third delivery onwards, the entitlement is reduced to 12 weeks.

Employees expecting a child should notify HR at least 30 days before the anticipated delivery date or at the earliest possible time. Notification must include the expected date of delivery certified by a registered medical practitioner. Early notification allows HR and the department to plan for coverage and transition.

Maternity leave can be taken partly before and partly after the delivery, with a mandatory minimum of 6 weeks to be availed after the delivery. The pre-delivery leave is subject to the employee's medical condition and is entirely at her discretion. HR will work closely with the employee to plan the leave schedule.

During maternity leave, the employee is entitled to full pay and all applicable benefits. Health insurance, PF contributions, and other statutory benefits continue uninterrupted during this period. The company is committed to supporting women employees through their maternity journey without any financial disadvantage.

Upon return from maternity leave, the employee is entitled to be reinstated in the same or equivalent role with the same compensation and designation. A transition support period of up to 2 weeks may be provided to ease the return to work. The company also offers a work-from-home option for up to 4 weeks post-return, subject to role suitability.

SECTION 6: PATERNITY LEAVE

Paternity leave is available to male employees or co-parents upon the birth of their child or the adoption of a child below 3 months of age. The entitlement is 5 working days, which must be availed within 30 days of the birth or adoption. It is available for the first two children only.

Paternity leave must be applied for at least 1 week in advance of the expected date, unless the birth occurs earlier than expected. Employees are required to submit a birth certificate or hospital discharge summary upon returning to work. The leave is granted on a paid basis and is not deducted from any other leave balance.

Paternity leave cannot be carried forward or encashed if not used within the stipulated 30-day window. The purpose of this leave is to allow the new parent to be present during the critical early days of the child's life. HR encourages all eligible employees to fully utilize this entitlement.

SECTION 7: BEREAVEMENT LEAVE

Bereavement leave is provided to employees who experience the loss of an immediate family member. Immediate family members include spouse, children, parents, siblings, and parents-in-law. The entitlement is 3 paid working days per bereavement event.

Employees must notify their manager and HR immediately upon the occurrence of a bereavement. Formal application for bereavement leave must be submitted in the HR portal within 3 days of return to work. Documentation such as a death certificate or an equivalent may be requested by HR.

For the loss of a more distant relative, such as grandparents, uncles, or aunts, employees may request 1 day of bereavement leave, subject to manager discretion. This day can also be covered by casual or annual leave if the bereavement leave entitlement has been exhausted. The organization treats bereavement with utmost sensitivity and compassion.

SECTION 8: PUBLIC HOLIDAYS

The company observes a list of national and regional public holidays as declared at the beginning of each calendar year. The list is published on the HR portal by December 31st of the preceding year. Employees are entitled to all mandatory national holidays as prescribed by law.

Regional offices may observe additional state or local public holidays based on the location of the office. Employees working at different locations will receive the applicable holiday list for their respective sites. Differences in holiday lists across offices are managed transparently by the HR department.

If a public holiday falls on a weekend, a compensatory holiday is provided on the nearest working day. Employees required to work on a public holiday due to business needs must receive either a compensatory day off or overtime pay as per applicable law. All such arrangements must be pre-approved and documented by HR.

SECTION 9: LEAVE WITHOUT PAY

Leave Without Pay (LWP) may be granted when an employee has exhausted all applicable leave balances. LWP is not an entitlement and is subject to manager and HR approval based on business requirements. It is intended for genuine personal emergencies or medical situations where no other leave is available.

Applications for LWP must be submitted in writing with a detailed justification. The maximum continuous period of LWP that can be granted is 30 calendar days. Extensions beyond this period require approval from the HR Director and the Business Unit Head.

LWP has implications on benefits such as PF contributions, which are calculated on actual days worked. The salary for the period of LWP is deducted from the monthly payroll on a proportional basis. Employees are advised to understand the financial and statutory implications before opting for LWP.

SECTION 10: COMPENSATORY LEAVE

Compensatory leave is granted to employees who are required to work on scheduled days off, weekends, or public holidays. The employee is entitled to claim compensatory time off equivalent to the additional hours worked. Compensatory leave must be claimed within 60 days of the date it was earned.

To claim compensatory leave, the employee must submit a request through the HR portal citing the date of extra work and the number of hours worked. Approval must be obtained from the reporting manager before the leave is credited to the balance. Unclaimed compensatory leave beyond the 60-day window will lapse without further entitlement.

Compensatory leave is not transferable or encashable under any circumstances. It is intended solely for the rest and recovery of the employee who performed extra work. Misrepresentation of compensatory leave claims will be treated as a disciplinary offense.

SECTION 11: ADOPTION LEAVE

Employees who legally adopt a child below the age of 3 months are entitled to adoption leave. Female employees are entitled to 12 weeks of adoption leave, while male employees are entitled to 5 working days. Adoption leave must be supported by official adoption documentation issued by a recognized adoption authority.

Adoption leave must be applied for at least 2 weeks before the anticipated adoption date, wherever possible. HR will work with the employee on an individual basis to accommodate the unique circumstances of adoption. All adoption-related information shared with HR is treated strictly confidentially.

SECTION 12: LEAVE DURING NOTICE PERIOD

Employees who have resigned or been issued a termination notice are generally not permitted to take annual leave during the notice period unless approved by the reporting manager and HR. Earned but unused leave may be encashed as part of the final settlement. Sick leave taken during the notice period requires medical documentation.

Casual leave may be granted during the notice period at the manager's discretion, provided it does not disrupt the handover process. The organization reserves the right to adjust the final settlement amount for leave taken in excess of entitlements during the notice period. Employees are encouraged to plan their leave usage prior to tendering resignation.

SECTION 13: LEAVE FOR RELIGIOUS AND CULTURAL OBSERVANCES

Employees may apply for leave to observe significant religious festivals, cultural events, or personal rites of passage. Such leave can be availed from the employee's annual or casual leave balance. The company encourages sensitivity toward diverse religious and cultural practices within the team.

Employees belonging to minority religious communities may apply for optional holidays from the approved optional holiday list. Each employee is permitted to select up to 2 optional holidays per calendar year in addition to the standard public holiday list. Optional holidays must be applied for at least 3 days in advance through the HR portal.

Managers are expected to be sensitive when reviewing leave requests related to religious or cultural observances. Denial of such leave without valid operational justification may be escalated to the HR Business Partner for review. The company is committed to fostering an inclusive environment that respects diverse belief systems.

SECTION 14: LEAVE ENCASHMENT

Leave encashment allows eligible employees to convert unused annual leave into a cash payment under defined circumstances. This benefit is available only to permanent employees who have completed at least one year of service. Contractual and probationary employees are not eligible for leave encashment.

Leave encashment during employment is permitted only once per calendar year and is capped at a maximum of 5 days per year. The encashment is calculated on the basis of the employee's current basic salary and is processed through the monthly payroll. The encashment request must be submitted to HR by the 20th of the month for inclusion in that month's payroll run.

Upon resignation or termination, eligible employees are entitled to encash all accumulated annual leave not exceeding the carry-forward limit. Encashment on separation is processed as part of the full and final settlement. Employees in probation or under a Performance Improvement Plan at the time of separation are not eligible for leave encashment.

SECTION 15: STUDY AND EXAMINATION LEAVE

Employees who are pursuing recognized part-time or distance learning educational programs may apply for study leave to prepare for examinations. A maximum of 2 days of study leave per examination may be granted, subject to availability and manager approval. Study leave is drawn from the employee's annual leave balance.

Proof of enrollment in the educational program and the examination schedule must be submitted to HR at least 2 weeks before the examination date. The leave will be approved only for examinations that are directly related to the employee's current or future role at the organization. Study leave requests that do not meet this criterion will be treated as regular annual leave.

SECTION 16: LEAVE FOR VOLUNTEER AND COMMUNITY SERVICE

The organization encourages employees to contribute to society through volunteering and community service activities. Employees are entitled to 2 days of paid volunteer leave per calendar year to participate in NGO-driven or company-organized social initiatives. Volunteer leave does not carry over and is not encashable.

Employees who wish to take volunteer leave must provide details of the volunteering activity, the organizing body, and the expected dates of participation. Approval is subject to manager availability assessment and HR validation of the organization. Post-event, employees are encouraged to share their experience through the company's internal social channel.

SECTION 17: LEAVE RECORDS AND AUDIT

All leave transactions are recorded in real time in the HR information system and are accessible to employees, managers, and HR at all times. Employees are advised to regularly review their leave balances and report any discrepancies to HR within 5 working days of the error being noticed. Delayed reporting of discrepancies may make corrections difficult to process retroactively.

The HR department conducts a quarterly audit of leave records to ensure accuracy and policy compliance. Any anomalies identified during the audit, such as excess leave approvals or unrecorded absences, are investigated and corrected. Audit findings are shared with department heads to promote awareness and adherence.

SECTION 18: POLICY REVISION AND EMPLOYEE ACKNOWLEDGMENT

This leave policy is reviewed annually by the HR team in consultation with legal counsel and senior management. Updates to the policy are driven by changes in labor law, business needs, or feedback from employee surveys. All revisions are communicated to employees at least 30 days before they take effect.

Employees are required to acknowledge receipt and understanding of the leave policy upon joining and each time the policy is updated. Acknowledgment is recorded electronically through the HR portal. Failure to acknowledge the policy within the stipulated deadline may result in a reminder from the HR Business Partner and, in persistent cases, a formal escalation.

Questions and clarifications regarding this policy may be directed to the HR helpdesk, which is available from Monday to Friday during business hours. HR is committed to providing clear and timely responses to all policy-related queries. Employees are also encouraged to attend the quarterly HR town halls where policy updates and clarifications are discussed.

SECTION 19: LEAVE MANAGEMENT FOR MANAGERS

Managers play a critical role in ensuring that leave is managed responsibly within their teams. They are expected to approve or reject leave requests within 48 hours of submission, providing clear reasons for any rejection. Managers must ensure adequate team coverage at all times, especially during peak business periods, project deadlines, and critical deliverable windows.

When multiple team members apply for leave on overlapping dates, managers must apply a fair and transparent process for determining approval priority. Factors such as the order of application, project needs, and prior approvals given should guide the decision. Managers must document their rationale for rejecting overlapping leave requests.

Managers must not pressure employees to cancel or postpone approved leave unless there is a genuine and documented operational emergency. If a recall from leave is necessary, the employee must be given at least 48 hours of advance notice. The organization will compensate employees for any direct costs incurred due to a leave recall, such as non-refundable travel bookings.

Managers are prohibited from discouraging employees from taking entitled leave or suggesting that leave usage will negatively impact performance ratings. Such behavior constitutes misuse of managerial authority and must be reported to HR. The organization believes that rested employees perform better and is committed to protecting employees' right to take their entitled leave.

SECTION 20: GLOSSARY OF LEAVE TERMS

Annual Leave (Privilege Leave / PL): Paid leave granted to permanent employees for personal rest and recreation, accruing at 1.5 days per month. Carry-forward is permitted up to 9 days per calendar year. Encashable on resignation or retirement.

Sick Leave (SL): Paid leave granted to employees who are unable to work due to illness or injury. Entitlement is 12 days per year. Medical certification is required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive days.

Casual Leave (CL): Paid leave for short unplanned personal requirements. Entitlement is 6 days per year, non-carryforward and non-encashable. Maximum of 2 consecutive days at a time.

Maternity Leave (ML): Statutory paid leave for female employees upon childbirth or adoption. Entitlement is 26 weeks for first two deliveries. Fully paid as per the Maternity Benefit Act.

Leave Without Pay (LWP): Unpaid leave granted when all leave balances are exhausted. Subject to management and HR approval. Impacts salary, benefits, and statutory contributions proportionally.

Compensatory Leave (CompOff): Leave granted in exchange for working on official rest days, weekends, or public holidays. Must be claimed within 60 days. Non-encashable and non-transferable.

Optional Holiday (OH): Leave selected by the employee from a prescribed list of optional festivals and observances. Maximum of 2 days per year per employee. Must be pre-applied and approved.

All defined terms in this glossary are consistent with their usage throughout this policy document. In case of ambiguity, the definitions herein shall take precedence. Employees may contact HR for clarification on any term or leave category.
